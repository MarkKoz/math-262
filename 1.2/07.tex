There are 6 possible types of a $2 \times 3$ matrix in reduced row-echelon form:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \begin{flalign*}
    & \begin{bmatrix}
      1 & 0 & a_{13} \\
      0 & 1 & a_{23} \\
    \end{bmatrix} &
  \end{flalign*}

  \item \begin{flalign*}
    & \begin{bmatrix}
      1 & a_{12} & 0 \\
      0 & 0 & 1 \\
    \end{bmatrix} &
  \end{flalign*}

  \item \begin{flalign*}
    & \begin{bmatrix}
      1 & a_{12} & a_{13} \\
      0 & 0 & 0 \\
    \end{bmatrix} &
  \end{flalign*}

  \item \begin{flalign*}
    & \begin{bmatrix}
      0 & 1 & 0 \\
      0 & 0 & 1 \\
    \end{bmatrix} &
  \end{flalign*}

  \item \begin{flalign*}
    & \begin{bmatrix}
      0 & 1 & a_{13} \\
      0 & 0 & 0 \\
    \end{bmatrix} &
  \end{flalign*}

  \item \begin{flalign*}
    & \begin{bmatrix}
      0 & 0 & 1 \\
      0 & 0 & 0 \\
    \end{bmatrix} &
  \end{flalign*}
\end{enumerate}
