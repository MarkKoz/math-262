\item This problem can be expressed as
\begin{align*}
  \begin{bmatrix}
    -0.5 & 1 & 0 \\
    -0.5 & 0 & 1 \\
  \end{bmatrix}
  \begin{bmatrix}
    x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\
  \end{bmatrix} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    0 \\ 0 \\
  \end{bmatrix}
\end{align*}
It can then be turned into a system of equations:
\begin{align*}
  \systeme{
    -0.5x_1 + x_2 = 0,
    -0.5x_1 + x_3 = 0
  } \longrightarrow
  \systeme{
    -0.5x_1 + x_2 = 0,
    x_2 - x_3 = 0
  }
\end{align*}
Notice that $x_2 = x_3$. Let $x_2 = x_3 = r$. Solving for $x_1$,
\begin{align*}
  -0.5x_1 + r & = 0               \\
  -0.5x_1     & = -r              \\
  x_1         & = \frac{-r}{-0.5} \\
  x_1         & = \frac{r}{0.5}   \\
  x_1         & = 2r              \\
\end{align*}
Thus
\begin{align*}
  \begin{bmatrix}
  x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\
  \end{bmatrix} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
  0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\
  \end{bmatrix} +
  \begin{bmatrix}
  2r \\ r \\ r \\
  \end{bmatrix} = r
  \begin{bmatrix}
  2 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\
  \end{bmatrix}
\end{align*}

\end{enumerate}
